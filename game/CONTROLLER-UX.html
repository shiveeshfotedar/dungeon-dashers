Phone as Controller

Custom Input Design

Leveraging touchscreens to create a card-based input system that transforms every smartphone into a personalized game controller.

The Concept

Traditional controllers are fixed hardware with predetermined buttons. A phone's touchscreen is a blank canvas. We can design any interface we want - and for a card game, we design the interface to BE the cards themselves.

The Input IS the Content

Instead of pressing "A" to play a card, you tap the actual card. The input mechanism and the game content are unified. This creates direct manipulation - what you see is what you interact with.

Context-Sensitive Interface

The controller changes based on game state. During your turn, you see your hand. Between turns, you see results. The interface always shows exactly what's relevant.

Rich Visual Feedback

Cards glow, animate, and respond to touch. Color-coded types (attack/defense/skill/utility) provide instant recognition. Stats are visible without memorization.

Private Information

Your hand is only on your screen. Other players can't see your cards until you play them. This enables strategy, bluffing, and the joy of surprise when plays are revealed.

Phone vs Traditional Controller

Phones offer unique advantages over traditional controllers for social party games, especially those involving hidden information and complex inputs.

Design Principle: Two-Step Confirmation

Playing a card requires two taps: focus then play. This prevents accidental plays on a touchscreen where mis-taps are common. The focused state gives you a chance to see the card details and confirm your choice before committing.

Default State

Cards sit slightly scaled down in the hand. Readable but not prominent. Encourages scanning and selection rather than immediate action.

Focused State

Selected card scales up with enhanced glow and brighter border. Play button appears. Clear visual distinction from siblings. Only one card focused at a time.

Playing Animation

Card slides upward and fades as it's "thrown" into battle. Provides satisfying feedback that the action registered.

Spent State

Card slot becomes empty/grayed. Visual confirmation that you've played this turn. Prevents confusion about whether your play was submitted.

Color-Coded Types

Red = Attack, Blue = Defense, Green = Skill, Purple = Utility. Instant recognition without reading. Players learn the colors in one game.

Card Anatomy

Each card shows: name, art frame, type badge, stat values, and play button. All information visible at a glance during the 10-second turn timer.

No Button Mapping

Traditional games: "Press X to attack." Here: Tap the attack card. The action is self-describing. New players understand instantly.

Visual Strategy

You can see your options. Comparing card stats is visual, not mental. Decision-making happens by looking, not remembering button combos.

Emergent Communication

"I have two attacks!" Players naturally share card information verbally. The hidden hand creates conversation and collaboration.

Satisfying Feedback

Tapping a beautifully rendered card that animates away is more satisfying than pressing a generic button. The input feels like casting a spell.

Deck Building as Personalization

The controller includes a deck builder that lets players customize their card pool. This extends the input design - you're not just choosing what to play, but curating what options you'll have.

Premade Decks

New players can select from preset decks like "ember-ward" or "wind-surge". These are balanced starting points that teach card synergies through play.

Custom Deck Building

Experienced players can build custom decks by specifying card IDs. The deck is validated server-side (min 10, max 40, max 3 copies per card).

Local Storage

Decks are saved to the phone's localStorage. Your custom deck persists across sessions without creating an account.

Reward Integration

Defeating monsters earns reward cards that are added to your deck for subsequent battles. Your deck grows as you progress.

Why This Matters

Deck building transforms input personalization from cosmetic (controller skins) to strategic. Your deck reflects your playstyle. Heavy attackers build damage. Support players stack heals. The controller becomes an expression of player identity.

Social Dynamics

The phone controller design creates unique social interactions that emerge from the combination of private screens and shared display.

"I've Got Healing!"

Players naturally announce what they have. "I can attack twice!" "Someone needs to defend!" The hidden hand creates conversation.

The Reveal Moment

When all cards are played, they appear on the TV simultaneously. "Yes! Team Strike!" The group reacts together to the combined plays.

Trust & Coordination

"I'll heal, you all attack." Players must trust each other to follow through. This builds social bonds through cooperative commitment.

Shared Tension

Everyone watches the same TV but holds different cards. The 10-second timer creates shared urgency as players coordinate in real-time.
