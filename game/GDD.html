Dungeon Dashers

Game Design Document

A cooperative card game that transforms your living room into a dungeon. The TV becomes your shared battlefield, phones become your weapons.

Core Vision

Dungeon Dashers reimagines the social gaming experience by leveraging devices everyone already owns. The TV serves as the communal focal point where all players share the same view of the dungeon, while personal phones act as private controllers where each player manages their hand of cards.

The Social Experience

Unlike online multiplayer where players are isolated behind screens, Dungeon Dashers brings people together in the same physical space. The TV creates a shared experience while phones enable private strategy.

BATTLE!
TV

Communal Focus

The TV anchors the experience. Everyone reacts to the same monster attacks, celebrates victories together, and feels the tension of low health as a group.

Phone

Private Strategy

Each player's hand is hidden on their phone. This creates moments of surprise when cards are revealed and opportunities for bluffing and coordination.

Talk

Real Communication

Players talk face-to-face. "I'll attack, you heal!" No voice chat delay, no typing. Just natural human coordination in a shared space.

No DL

Zero Friction

No app downloads required. Players simply open a URL on their phone browser. Join in seconds, play immediately.

Design Pillars

Three core principles guide every design decision in Dungeon Dashers.

Together
Cooperative Core
Players work together against the dungeon. There are no hidden agendas or traitors. Success requires coordination and communication. When one player falls, everyone feels it.
Fast
Simultaneous Action
All players act at once within 10-second turns. No waiting for your turn while others play. Everyone is always engaged, always making decisions.
Simple
Accessible Depth
Simple enough to learn in one round, deep enough to master over many games. Four card types, clear effects, but powerful combo potential.

Resolution Order

  1. Check for combos (multiple players coordinate)
  2. Apply player attacks to monster
  3. Monster attacks party (reduced by defense)
  4. Apply healing to party
  5. Increase monster rage meter

Rage Mechanic

Every monster has a rage meter that fills each turn. When it reaches 100, the monster unleashes a devastating attack, dealing double damage to the party.

How Rage Works

  • Rage increases by monster's rage value each turn
  • Different monsters fill rage at different rates
  • At 100 rage: monster deals double attack damage
  • After rage attack, meter resets to 0

Countering Rage

  • Focus card reduces rage by 20
  • Kill monster before rage fills
  • Coordinate defense for rage turn
  • Dragon Lord's "Rage Burst" is especially dangerous
